Grilled Salmon

Ingredients
- 1 1/2 pounds salmon fillets
- 1/3 cup soy sauce
- lemon peper to taste
- 1/3 cup water
- garlic powder to taste
- salt to taste
- 1/4 cup vegetable oil

Direction 1

Grilled Salmon
1. Season salmon fillets with lemon pepper, garlic powder, and salt.
2. In a small bowl, stir together soy sauce, brown sugar, water, and vegetable oil until sugar is dissolved. Place fish in a large resealable plastic bag with the soy sauce mixture, seal, and turn to coat. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours.

Direction 2

Grilled Salmon
3. Preheat grill for medium heat.
4. Lightly oil grill grate. Place salmon on the preheated grill, and discard marinade. Cook salmon for 6 to 8 minutes per side, or until the fish flakes easily with a fork.

Easy Meatloaf

Ingredients
- 1 1/2 pounds ground beef
- 1 egg
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1 cup milk
- 1 cup dried bread crumbs
- salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar
- 2 tablespoons preapared mestard
- 1/3 cup ketchup

Direction 1

Easy Meatloaf
1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
2. In a large bowl, combine the beef, egg, onion, milk and bread OR cracker crumbs. Season with salt and pepper to taste and place in a lightly greased 5x9 inch loaf pan, OR form into a loaf and place in a lightly greased 9x13 inch baking dish.

Direction 2

Easy Meatloaf
4. In a separate small bowl, combine the brown sugar, mustard and ketchup. Mix well and pour over the meatloaf.
5. Bake at 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) for 1 hour.

Garlic Butter Steak

Ingredients
- 2 tablespoons butter,softened, divided
- 1 teaspoon minced fresh parsley
- 1/2 teaspoon mince garlic
- 1/4 teaspoon reduced-sodium soy sauce
- 1 beef flat iron steak or bonelees top sirloin steak
- 1/8 teaspoon salt
- 1/8 teaspoon pepper

Direction 1

Garlic Butter Steak
1. Mix 1 tablespoon butter, parsley, garlic and soy sauce.

Direction 2

Garlic Butter Steak
2. Sprinkle steak with salt and pepper. In a large skillet, heat remaining butter over medium heat. Add steak; cook until meat reaches desired doneness (for medium-rare, a thermometer should read 135°; medium, 140°; medium-well, 145°), 4-7 minutes per side. Serve with garlic butter.
